Nuclear Is Not What You Think

Most people hear "nuclear" and think bombs or Chernobyl. Reality: nuclear technology touches your life every day. Your doctor uses it, your food is safer because of it, and the infrastructure that powers Texas relies on it. This module shows you the full picture.

Key concept: Nuclear technology is not one thing. It's a family of tools: fission reactors, radiation sources, detectors, isotope production facilities. All built and maintained by people with exactly your skills.

Where Nuclear Technology Is Used

Nuclear technology appears in 6 major application areas. Click each card to explore how nuclear technology works in that domain.

⚡ Power Generation
Click to expand
94 reactors across the US generate ~20% of our electricity. Nuclear provides 24/7 baseload power. It doesn't depend on wind, sun, or fuel deliveries. When the grid needs reliable power, nuclear delivers. (Source: EIA, 2024)
🏥 Medical Imaging & Treatment
Click to expand
PET scans use positron-emitting isotopes to image metabolic activity. Radiation therapy kills cancer tumors while sparing healthy tissue. The US performs ~40 million nuclear medicine procedures per year. (Source: SNMMI)
🍎 Food Safety
Click to expand
Irradiation uses gamma rays (typically Co-60 sources) or electron beams to kill pathogens in food without chemicals or making it radioactive. FDA-approved for spices, produce, poultry, and meat. (Source: FDA, CDC)
🔧 Industrial Inspection (NDT)
Click to expand
Radiography inspects welds and pipelines without cutting them open. Uses gamma sources (Ir-192, Co-60) or X-rays to image internal structures. Every safety-related weld at a nuclear plant gets RT or UT inspection. (Source: ASNT)
🚀 Space Exploration
Click to expand
RTGs (radioisotope thermoelectric generators) use heat from Pu-238 decay to generate electricity. No moving parts. Powers Voyager (launched 1977, still operating), Curiosity, and Perseverance rovers on Mars. (Source: NASA/DOE)
🛢 Resource Exploration
Click to expand
Well logging uses radiation detectors lowered into drill holes to measure rock formations. Helps identify oil and gas deposits without drilling exploratory wells. Nuclear technology makes energy exploration more efficient. (Source: SPE)

Why Nuclear Needs YOU

The nuclear workforce is aging out and new construction is ramping up. The industry needs skilled trades workers: electricians, pipefitters, welders, millwrights, and I&C technicians. You already have the core skills. This course adds only the nuclear layer.

375,000
Net new workers needed by 2050 (DOE Liftoff Report 2024)
40%
Current nuclear workforce retiring within a decade
10,000
Skilled craft workers needed for new construction by 2030
$102,674

The key insight: Nuclear plants don't run on nuclear physicists. They run on electricians, pipefitters, welders, millwrights, and I&C technicians. You already have the core skills. This course adds only the nuclear layer.
