### Module-by-Module Assessment

#### Module 1: Welcome to Nuclear Technology ✅ STRONG
**Coverage**: Nuclear applications (medical, energy, NDT, food, space, resource exploration), energy density, workforce stats, career path explorer
**Interactives**: "Is This Nuclear?" classifier, Energy Density Visualizer (vs coal/gas/oil/wind), Career Path Explorer (4 trades)
**Quiz Quality**: Good — tests application identification, nuclear medicine stats, workforce numbers, NDT definition
**Strengths**: Excellent demystification of nuclear, surprising applications (smoke detectors, bananas, exit signs), workforce stats sourced (DOE Liftoff Report 2024)
**Gaps**: No mention of new reactor builds (SMR context), Texas focus could be broader
**Pedagogical grade**: A

#### Module 2: How Nuclear Reactors Work ✅ STRONG
**Coverage**: Fission, chain reactions, control rods, PWR vs BWR, energy path, capacity factor
**Interactives**: Chain Reaction Simulator (neutron firing + control rod slider), PWR vs BWR Flow Diagram, Control Rod Challenge (drag-to-target power), Click-Through Plant Tour
**Quiz Quality**: Excellent — tests understanding not just recall. Q5 (capacity factor 90-93%) and Q6 (turbine building = most familiar for electricians) are particularly good
**Strengths**: Four interactives is the highest of any module; chain reaction sim with SCRAM capability is standout
**Gaps**: No SMR content, no mention of currently-under-construction plants (Vogtle 3&4 context, BWRX-300)
**Pedagogical grade**: A

#### Module 3: Radiation Safety Fundamentals ✅ SOLID
**Coverage**: 4 radiation types, dose units (rem/mrem/Sv), NRC limits, ALARA, contamination vs radiation
**Interactives**: Radiation Penetration Simulator (canvas-based, 4 types), Dose Comparison Explorer, ALARA Planning Challenge
**Quiz Quality**: Strong — Q4 (inverse square law) and Q5 (radiation vs contamination distinction) are exactly what a worker needs to know
**Strengths**: The contamination vs. radiation distinction (Q5) is critical for daily work — often misunderstood
**Gaps**: No mention of dosimetry devices (TLD, EPD), no contamination control procedures, no mention of GET/NANTeL (access training prerequisite)
**Pedagogical grade**: A-

#### Module 4: Inside the Reactor ✅ STRONG
**Coverage**: Reactor vessel components, safety systems (ECCS, containment, EDGs), Defense in Depth (5 barriers), reactor control (SCRAM), trades maintenance roles
**Interactives**: Reactor Vessel Cutaway Explorer (canvas), Defense in Depth Builder (drag-order barriers), Decay Heat Cooldown Simulator (Normal/Loss of Cooling/Fukushima scenarios)
**Quiz Quality**: Excellent — Q4 (Fukushima: SCRAM worked but cooling lost) demonstrates applied understanding over rote knowledge. Q5 (Class 1E) and Q6 (EDG 10-second start) are high-value workforce knowledge
**Strengths**: Fukushima scenario in the cooldown simulator is pedagogically brilliant — safety lesson without lecture
**Gaps**: No regulatory compliance content (10 CFR 50.49, 50.65, 50.59 — all covered in prior electrician demo analysis), no QA/human performance content
**Pedagogical grade**: A

#### Module 5: Your Career Path ✅ PRACTICAL
**Coverage**: Trade-to-nuclear translation, workforce shortage, salary comparison, qualification pathway (7-step), background investigation detail
**Interactives**: Career Path Explorer (4 trades, personalized paths), Salary Calculator (trade + experience slider, commercial vs nuclear comparison), Qualification Roadmap (7-step interactive checklist)
**Quiz Quality**: Good — Q1 (40-55% wage premium) and Q3 (background investigation 4-12 weeks) give workers accurate expectations. Q4 (NANTeL Passport portability) is valuable practical knowledge
**Strengths**: The 7-step qualification roadmap is exactly what a motivated trades worker needs to see — concrete, actionable
**Gaps**: Nuclear Talent Scout ClickUp link may not be live for public demo; salary data should cite source year; no mention of union (IBEW, UA) pathways; background investigation Q6 ("minor offenses rarely disqualify — disclose upfront") is excellent but needs "consult HR before applying" caveat
**Pedagogical grade**: B+

#### Module 6: Citations & Sources ✅ EXCELLENT TRUST-BUILDER
**Coverage**: 32 sources across 6 categories (Textbooks, Regulatory, Industry, Academic/MIT OCW, Government, Web Resources)
**Features**: Full-text search, category filtering, APA-format copy buttons, course completion banner, MIT OCW attribution banner
**Notable sources**: Lamarsh & Baratta (textbook standard), 10 CFR Parts 20/50/73.56, NEI 2024 Workforce Report, IAEA Fukushima Report, BLS Occupational Outlook
**Strengths**: This module alone differentiates Reactor Ready from low-credibility online "nuclear training" content. A DOE or utility reviewer will respect this immediately.
**Gaps**: No INPO sources cited, no ACAD references, no union/labor sources
**Pedagogical grade**: A

### Pedagogical Flow Assessment (1 → 6)

```
M1 (Why nuclear? Awareness)
  → M2 (How it works: Technical foundation)
    → M3 (Safety: Essential knowledge)
      → M4 (Inside the plant: Deep technical)
        → M5 (Your career: Motivational close)
          → M6 (Sources: Credibility anchor)
```

**Assessment**: The flow is **logically sound and pedagogically appropriate**. It follows the classic "engage → teach → equip → inspire → validate" arc. Each module builds on the prior. A trades worker who completes all 6 has:
1. Overcome nuclear fear/myths (M1)
2. Understood what they'll actually work on (M2, M4)
3. Learned the safety culture they'll live in (M3)
4. Seen how the plant they'll maintain actually works (M4)
5. Understood their career path and compensation (M5)
6. Can point to credible sources when questioned (M6)

**One gap in the flow**: Between M4 (technical deep dive) and M5 (career), there's no bridge to **regulatory culture** (why nuclear is different from commercial work). This was identified in the prior electrician demo analysis. Adding a Module 4.5 or expanding M4 to cover 10 CFR basics, SAT methodology, and work control would strengthen the transition from "I understand the plant" to "I understand how nuclear work gets done."

The single largest barrier to NRST grant funding is the lack of an industry-recognized credential. Options:

1. **Partner with a community college** to offer modules as credit-bearing coursework → college issues certificate
2. **NCCER nuclear pathway** — NCCER already offers nuclear training credentials; content could map to NCCER nuclear learning objectives
3. **Work with EPRI or NEI** to develop a "Pre-Employment Nuclear Awareness Certificate" that utilities would recognize
4. **Partner with NANTeL** — NANTeL Passport is already cross-utility recognized; if content feeds into NANTeL pre-employment modules, graduates would have a portable credential
